THEKKADY

Periyar Tiger Reserve, commonly known as Thekkady, is a nature lover's paradise nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India. It is renowned for its wildlife sanctuary and scenic beauty, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and wildlife aficionados. The highlight of Thekkady is the Periyar National Park, a vast protected area that spans over 900 square kilometers. The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, deer, leopards, and numerous species of birds. One of the main attractions is the boat safari on Periyar Lake, where visitors can spot wildlife, especially elephants, grazing along the banks. Trekking and nature walks are also available, providing opportunities to explore the dense forests and enjoy the serenity of the surroundings. Apart from the wildlife, Thekkady is known for its spice plantations. The region is abundant in cardamom, pepper, cinnamon, and other aromatic spices. Visitors can take a guided tour through the spice gardens, learn about the cultivation and processing techniques, and even purchase fresh spices directly from the plantations. Thekkady is also famous for its traditional art forms and cultural heritage. Kathakali, a classical dance-drama, and Kalaripayattu, an ancient martial art form, are regularly performed in various venues around Thekkady. These art forms showcase the rich cultural heritage of Kerala and offer visitors a chance to witness the vibrant traditions of the region. With its abundant wildlife, lush greenery, spice plantations, and cultural experiences, Thekkady captivates visitors with its natural and cultural treasures. It is an ideal destination for those seeking a blend of adventure, tranquility, and an immersive encounter with the wonders of nature.
