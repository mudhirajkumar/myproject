KUNDREMUKH

Kudremukh is a small hill station located in the Chikmagalur district of Karnataka state in India. The name "Kudremukh" literally means "horse-face" in the local Kannada language, as the shape of the mountain resembles the face of a horse. The region is known for its natural beauty, trekking trails, and biodiversity, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts. One of the major attractions of Kudremukh is the Kudremukh National Park, which was established in 1987 and is spread over an area of 600 square kilometers. The park is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including several endangered species such as the lion-tailed macaque, the Malabar civet, and the great Indian hornbill. The park also offers several trekking trails that take visitors through dense forests, rolling hills, and picturesque waterfalls. Apart from its natural beauty, Kudremukh is also known for its mining industry. The Kudremukh Iron Ore Company Limited (KIOCL) operated a large iron ore mine in the region, which was closed in 2006 due to environmental concerns. Today, efforts are being made to revive the region's economy through sustainable tourism and other industries that do not harm the environment. Overall, Kudremukh is a beautiful destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, wildlife, and industrial history. Visitors to the region can enjoy trekking, camping, and other outdoor activities while also learning about the region's rich cultural and ecological heritage.
