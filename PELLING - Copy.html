PELLING

Pelling is a quaint hill station nestled in the beautiful state of Sikkim, in East India. It is renowned for its stunning views of the snow-capped peaks of Mount Kanchenjunga, the third highest mountain in the world. The town offers a serene and peaceful atmosphere, making it an ideal getaway for nature lovers and those seeking tranquility. Pelling is dotted with numerous monasteries that add to its spiritual charm, including the famous Pemayangtse Monastery, which is one of the oldest and most significant monasteries in Sikkim. Apart from its breathtaking mountain vistas, Pelling also boasts enchanting waterfalls that cascade down from the hills, creating a mesmerizing sight. The most popular one is the Khecheopalri Lake, also known as the "Wishing Lake," which is considered sacred by both Buddhists and Hindus. The lake is surrounded by lush green forests, making it a picturesque spot for nature walks and picnics. Pelling is also a great base for adventure enthusiasts, offering opportunities for trekking, mountain biking, and exploring the surrounding areas. In addition to its natural beauty, Pelling provides a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Sikkim. The local inhabitants are warm and welcoming, and you can experience their vibrant traditions and customs firsthand. The town has a range of accommodation options, from cozy guesthouses to luxury resorts, ensuring a comfortable stay for visitors. Overall, Pelling is a hidden gem in East India, offering a serene retreat in the lap of the Himalayas, with its scenic landscapes, cultural richness, and spiritual ambiance
